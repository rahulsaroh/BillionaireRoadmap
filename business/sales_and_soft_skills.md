# Closer’s Playbook: Sales & Communication Skills

This playbook covers soft skills, active listening frameworks, "The Mom Test" diagnostics, and camera drills designed for tech-minded founders.

---

## 1. The Core Philosophy
- **"The best product rarely wins; the product that sells the best always wins."**
- True B2B sales is not about manipulative talking—it is about **empathy, identifying leakage points, and problem-solving**.
- Shift conversations away from *features* (tech stack, databases, UI options) to *outcomes* (saving money, plugging leaks, automating operations).

---

## 2. Recommended Reading & Core Frameworks

### I. *The Mom Test* (Rob Fitzpatrick)
- **Concept**: Never ask customers if your app is a good idea. They will lie to be polite.
- **Rule**: Investigate past behavior and current headaches instead of future intentions.
- **Gym Pitch Diagnostics**:
  - *Do Not Ask*: "Would you pay for a dashboard that shows membership renewals?"
  - *Ask Instead*: "When was the last time a client dropped off because their package expired without anyone noticing? What did that cost you?"

### II. *Never Split the Difference* (Chris Voss)
- **Concept**: Use FBI-negotiation tactics to build safety and control for the prospect.
- **Rule**: Master vocal inflection and labeling.
- **Vocal Tone**: Inflect voice downward at the end of sentences ("Late-Night DJ Voice") to establish authority and trust.

### III. *SPIN Selling* (Neil Rackham)
- **Concept**: Use a sequence of questions to guide a prospect to realize their own pain points.
- **Sequence**:
  1. **S**ituation: Establish their current setup.
  2. **P**roblem: Find where it is failing.
  3. **I**mplication: Explore what happens if it is not fixed (financial losses, leaks).
  4. **N**eed-Payoff: Let them explain how much they need the solution.

## 3. Weekend Camera Drill Protocols
Record yourself on webcam or smartphone for 15 minutes to analyze body language, filler words, and vocal pacing.

### Drill 1: The "Late-Night DJ Voice" Inflection
- **Take 1 (Baseline)**: Speak fast, matching high-stress pacing, using upward inflection at the end of sentences (making pitches sound like questions).
- **Take 2 (Negotiator)**: Drop your voice an octave, slow down pacing by 20%, and hit downward inflections at the end of sentences.
- **Review**: Note the contrast in perceived authority.

### Drill 2: SPIN Implication Script
- **Scenario**: A gym manager states: *"Our personal trainers track sessions in a notebook."*
- **Practice (2 Mins)**: Address the camera lens directly with:
  > *"When a trainer forgets to write down a session in that notebook, how much revenue do you think leaks out at the end of the month? If that happens across 5 trainers, does that mean you are losing tens of thousands of rupees every single quarter?"*
- **Review**: Keep eyes relaxed and hold steady eye contact with the camera lens without blinking or looking away.
